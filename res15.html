Ramen

Here's a simple and delicious ramen recipe you can try at home! It’s a basic miso ramen with a flavorful broth, but feel free to customize it with your favorite toppings.

Ingredients:
For the Broth:
4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
1 tablespoon miso paste (white or red, depending on preference)
1 tablespoon soy sauce
1 tablespoon sesame oil
1 teaspoon ginger (freshly grated or powdered)
1 clove garlic (minced)
1 teaspoon rice vinegar (optional)
1 tablespoon mirin (optional)
For the Ramen:
2 packs of ramen noodles (or any noodles you prefer)
Toppings (Optional but recommended):
2 soft-boiled eggs (sliced in half)
1 cup cooked chicken or pork (shredded or sliced thin)
1/2 cup corn kernels
1/2 cup spinach or bok choy
2 green onions (chopped)
Nori (seaweed sheets)
Bean sprouts
Sesame seeds
Chili oil or sriracha (for some spice)

Instructions:
1.Prepare the Broth: In a large pot, heat the sesame oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and ginger, and sauté for about 2 minutes, until fragrant. Add the chicken or vegetable broth, soy sauce, miso paste, rice vinegar, and mirin. Stir until the miso paste is fully dissolved and combined. Bring the broth to a simmer, then lower the heat and let it simmer gently while you prepare the noodles and toppings. Cook the Ramen Noodles:
2.In a separate pot, bring water to a boil and cook the ramen noodles according to the package instructions (usually 2-4 minutes). Drain the noodles and set them aside. Assemble the Ramen:
3.Divide the cooked noodles between two bowls. Pour the hot broth over the noodles in each bowl.
4.Add Toppings: Arrange your favorite toppings on top of the ramen. For example, place a halved soft-boiled egg, sliced meat, corn, spinach or bok choy, green onions, nori, and any other toppings you prefer.
5.Serve: Optionally, drizzle some chili oil or add sriracha for a spicy kick. Serve immediately while hot!
Enjoy your homemade ramen!
