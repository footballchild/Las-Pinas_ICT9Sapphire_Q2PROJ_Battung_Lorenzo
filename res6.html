Tamago Kake Gohan

Tamagokake Gohan (卵かけご飯) is a simple, traditional Japanese dish that consists of a raw egg mixed with hot rice, seasoned with soy sauce. It's comforting, quick, and delicious, often enjoyed for breakfast. Here's how you can make Tamagokake Gohan at home:

Ingredients:
1 cup short-grain or medium-grain rice (cooked and hot)
1 fresh egg (make sure it’s very fresh, since it will be consumed raw)
1-2 tsp soy sauce (to taste)
1/2 tsp mirin (optional, for a touch of sweetness)
A pinch of salt (optional)
Garnishes (optional): chopped green onions, sesame seeds, or nori (seaweed)

Instructions:
1. Cook the Rice: Cook the rice according to the package instructions or using a rice cooker. The rice should be freshly cooked and hot when you serve it for tamagokake gohan.
2. Prepare the Egg: Crack the egg into a small bowl. You can use a fresh egg directly from the shell. Some people choose to gently beat the egg with chopsticks or a fork to slightly break up the yolk.
3. Season the Egg: Add 1-2 teaspoons of soy sauce to the egg, and mix gently. If you prefer a slightly sweeter flavor, add 1/2 teaspoon of mirin (optional). You can also add a pinch of salt for extra flavor if desired.
4. Assemble the Tamagokake Gohan: Place the hot rice into a bowl. Pour the seasoned egg mixture over the hot rice. The heat from the rice will gently cook the egg, creating a creamy, silky texture when mixed.
5. Mix and Serve: Stir the egg mixture into the rice until fully incorporated. The egg will blend into the rice, giving it a smooth, rich texture.
6. Optional Garnishes: You can top your tamagokake gohan with chopped green onions, sesame seeds, or shredded nori (seaweed) for added flavor and texture.

Tips:
Freshness of the egg: Since the egg is raw, it is important to use the freshest egg possible. If you're concerned about raw eggs, consider using pasteurized eggs, which are safe to eat raw.
Rice: Make sure the rice is hot when you pour the egg over it, as this helps cook the egg slightly and gives the dish a smooth, creamy texture.
Customization: You can customize the dish by adding other toppings, like grated daikon, pickles, or a drizzle of sesame oil. Tamagokake Gohan is simple, comforting, and a perfect quick meal! Enjoy it as a breakfast, a snack, or even a light dinner.
