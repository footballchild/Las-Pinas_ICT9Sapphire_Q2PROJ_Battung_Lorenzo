Bacsilog

Bacsilog is a popular Filipino breakfast dish consisting of bacon (bac), sinangag (garlic fried rice), and a fried egg (itlog). It's a savory, flavorful, and hearty meal that is quick to prepare and perfect for starting the day. Here’s a simple recipe for Bacsilog:

Ingredients:
For the Bacon (Bac):
4-6 slices of bacon (preferably thick-cut)
1 tablespoon brown sugar (optional, for sweetness)
1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
1 tablespoon soy sauce (optional, for extra flavor)
For the Garlic Fried Rice (Sinangag):
2 cups cooked rice (preferably cold, day-old rice works best)
2 tablespoons vegetable oil or butter
3-4 cloves garlic (minced)
Salt to taste
For the Fried Egg:
2 large eggs
1 tablespoon oil (for frying)
Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:
1. Prepare the Bacon (Bac): In a pan over medium heat, cook the bacon slices until they are crispy and golden brown, about 5-7 minutes. Flip them halfway through to ensure even cooking. If you'd like your bacon to have a slight sweetness, sprinkle the bacon with 1 tablespoon of brown sugar in the last minute of cooking, then allow it to caramelize slightly. Once cooked, remove the bacon from the pan and place it on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess grease.
2. Make the Garlic Fried Rice (Sinangag): In the same pan, remove excess bacon fat, leaving about 1-2 tablespoons for flavor. If there’s not enough, add 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil or butter. Add the minced garlic and sauté over medium heat until fragrant and golden brown, about 1-2 minutes. Add the cold, day-old rice to the pan. Break up any clumps and stir-fry for 3-5 minutes until the rice is heated through and slightly crispy. Season the rice with salt to taste. If desired, you can also add a pinch of pepper. Once the rice is cooked and flavored, set it aside and keep it warm.
3. Fry the Eggs: In a separate pan, heat 1 tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Crack the eggs into the pan, and fry them sunny-side-up or to your preferred level of doneness. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper.
4. Assemble the Bacsilog: On a plate, serve a generous portion of garlic fried rice (sinangag). Place the cooked bacon on the side. Top with a fried egg. You can garnish with extra toppings like fried garlic chips or pickled vegetables (atsara) if desired.

Tips:
Rice: If you don’t have day-old rice, you can use freshly cooked rice, but it’s best to cool it down first to avoid a mushy texture when frying. Sweet bacon: If you prefer a sweeter flavor for your bacon, you can use a little honey or maple syrup instead of brown sugar for a more caramelized effect.
Customization: Add some sautéed vegetables like bell peppers or onions to the garlic fried rice for extra flavor and nutrients.
Bacsilog is a simple, satisfying meal that’s perfect for breakfast or any time you want a filling, flavorful dish. Enjoy this Filipino classic!
