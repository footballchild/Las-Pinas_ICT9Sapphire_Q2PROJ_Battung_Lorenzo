Halo-Halo

Halo-Halo is a beloved Filipino dessert, a colorful and refreshing mix of crushed ice, various sweetened fruits, jellies, beans, and other delicious toppings, all layered together and topped with leche flan, purple yam (ube), and sometimes ice cream. It's a perfect dessert to enjoy on hot days!

Halo-Halo Recipe
Ingredients:
For the Base (the mix-ins):
1 cup sweetened red beans (or kidney beans)
1 cup sweetened jackfruit (sliced)
1/2 cup sweetened banana (saba) slices in syrup
1/2 cup nata de coco (coconut gel), drained
1/2 cup macapuno (sweetened coconut sport), drained
1/2 cup sweetened mung beans (optional)
1/4 cup sweetened white or purple yam (ube) (optional, but recommended for the signature flavor)
1/2 cup shaved ice (or crushed ice)
1 tablespoon sugar (to taste, optional)
For the Toppings:
1 scoop ube ice cream (optional but highly recommended)
1-2 tablespoons leche flan (homemade or store-bought)
Evaporated milk (about 1/4 cup per serving)
Pinipig (toasted rice flakes, optional)

Instructions:
1.Prepare the Base Ingredients: Sweetened red beans: You can buy these canned or make them by simmering kidney beans with sugar until they are soft and sweet. Sweetened jackfruit: If using fresh jackfruit, slice and cook in sugar syrup. Canned or jarred sweetened jackfruit works fine as well. Banana (Saba): Slice the bananas and cook in syrup (or use canned sweetened bananas). Nata de coco and Macapuno: These are both coconut-based treats you can buy from the store, usually in jars. Sweetened mung beans and Ube: Both can be bought canned, or you can prepare them at home by cooking the mung beans and mashed ube with sugar and milk.
2.Assemble the Halo-Halo: In a tall glass or bowl, add a generous amount of sweetened beans, jackfruit, banana slices, nata de coco, macapuno, and mung beans. Add a few spoonfuls of the sweetened ube and a small layer of shaved ice on top. If you prefer, you can sprinkle a little sugar for extra sweetness at this stage (optional).
3.Add the Toppings: Add a scoop of ube ice cream on top of the shaved ice. Add slices of leche flan for a creamy, caramelized topping. Optionally, sprinkle some pinipig (toasted rice flakes) for extra crunch.
4.Pour the Milk: Drizzle evaporated milk over the top to bind everything together. The milk adds a creamy texture to the dish and ties the flavors together.
5.Mix and Enjoy: Mix it up! Halo-Halo is meant to be mixed together, so stir everything well to combine the different ingredients. The result will be a refreshing and colorful dessert with a variety of textures and flavors.
6.Serve Immediately: Serve your Halo-Halo with a spoon and straw, and enjoy the refreshing mix of flavors and textures!

Tips:
Customizing: Halo-Halo is highly customizable! You can add other ingredients like taro or pandan jelly depending on what you like.
Cold toppings: If you want, you can freeze the leche flan or ube ice cream beforehand for an even colder dessert.
Fresh fruits: Some variations of Halo-Halo use fresh fruits like mango, pineapple, or melon.
This Halo-Halo recipe is perfect for a hot day or for a special occasion with family and friends. It's fun, colorful, and packed with so many delicious flavors!
