Crispy Pata

Crispy Pata is a popular Filipino dish made of deep-fried pork knuckles (often served as a special treat for family gatherings or celebrations). It’s crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, typically served with a dipping sauce like vinegar with garlic, soy sauce, and chili. Here's a step-by-step recipe to make Crispy Pata:

Ingredients:
For the Pork Knuckles:
1 whole pork pata (pork knuckle, about 2-3 lbs)
1 medium onion, quartered
1 head garlic, halved
2 bay leaves
1 tablespoon peppercorns
1 teaspoon salt (for boiling)
1 tablespoon soy sauce (optional, for extra flavor)
Water (enough to submerge the pork)
For the Crispy Coating:
1 cup cornstarch
1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
1 teaspoon baking powder
Salt and pepper to taste
For Frying:
Vegetable oil (enough to fully submerge the pork)
For the Dipping Sauce:
1/4 cup vinegar
2 cloves garlic, minced
1 tablespoon soy sauce
1 small chili (optional)
Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:
1. Prepare the Pork Pata: Clean the pork knuckles: Wash the pork knuckles thoroughly, and use a knife or kitchen torch to remove any remaining hairs.
2. Boil the Pork Pata: In a large pot, add the pork knuckles, onion, garlic, bay leaves, peppercorns, and salt. Pour enough water to fully submerge the pork. Bring the water to a boil, then lower the heat and let it simmer for about 2-3 hours (or until the pork is tender but not falling apart). You may need to occasionally add more water to keep the pork submerged. Optionally, add a tablespoon of soy sauce for extra flavor during the boiling process. Once the pork is tender, remove it from the pot and let it cool down for a while. You can save the broth for other dishes or soups.
3. Prepare the Crispy Coating: Pat the pork knuckles dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture (this will help achieve a crispy exterior). In a shallow bowl, mix the cornstarch, flour, baking powder, salt, and pepper. Roll the boiled and dried pork knuckles in the coating mixture, ensuring that the pork is evenly covered.
4. Fry the Pork Pata: Heat vegetable oil in a large pot or deep fryer. You’ll need enough oil to fully submerge the pork knuckles. Once the oil reaches 350°F (175°C), carefully lower the coated pork knuckles into the hot oil. Fry for about 10-15 minutes, or until the skin turns golden brown and crispy. Be careful when frying, as the oil may splatter when you add the pork. Use tongs or a slotted spoon to remove the crispy pata from the oil and place it on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
5. Prepare the Dipping Sauce: In a small bowl, mix the vinegar, minced garlic, soy sauce, and chili (if using). Season with salt and pepper to taste. Stir the sauce well and set it aside for dipping.
6. Serve: Once the crispy pata is fried to perfection, transfer it to a serving platter. Slice the pork into manageable portions (using a cleaver or sharp knife) and serve with the dipping sauce on the side. You can serve it with steamed rice and a side of pickled vegetables (like atchara) for a complete meal.

Tips:
Boiling the Pork: Boiling the pork first is crucial as it tenderizes the meat, making it juicy inside while ensuring a crispy exterior when fried. You can boil it the day before and refrigerate it overnight for easier handling.
Frying: Make sure the oil is hot enough before frying to ensure the skin crisps up properly. Frying at the right temperature is key to achieving that perfect crunch.
Extra Crispy: For even crispier results, some people recommend freezing the boiled pork knuckles for a couple of hours before frying.
Crispy Pata is a fun and indulgent dish that pairs perfectly with family gatherings and special occasions. The crispy exterior and tender meat inside, combined with the vinegar-based dipping sauce, make it irresistible! Enjoy!
