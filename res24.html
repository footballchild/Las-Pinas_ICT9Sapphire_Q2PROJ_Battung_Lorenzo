Tea

Here's a simple recipe for tea! Whether you prefer black tea, green tea, or herbal tea, the process is similar, but each type has its own ideal brewing time and temperature.

Basic Tea Recipe (for any type of tea) Ingredients:
1 tea bag or 1 teaspoon loose-leaf tea
1 cup (240 ml) water
Milk, sugar, honey, or lemon (optional)

Instructions:
1.Boil the Water: Heat 1 cup of water to the appropriate temperature for your type of tea: Black tea: Boil the water (around 212°F or 100°C). Green tea: Heat the water to around 170-180°F (76-82°C). Herbal tea: Boil the water (around 212°F or 100°C).
2.Steep the Tea: Place the tea bag or loose-leaf tea in a cup or teapot. Pour the hot water over the tea bag or leaves. Let it steep for the recommended time: Black tea: 3-5 minutes. Green tea: 2-3 minutes. Herbal tea: 5-7 minutes (depending on the herb).
3.Remove the Tea Bag or Leaves: After steeping, remove the tea bag or strain out the loose leaves.
4.Customize Your Tea: Add milk, sugar, honey, or a slice of lemon to taste, if desired.
5.Serve and Enjoy: Stir and enjoy your freshly brewed cup of tea!

Tips:
Water Quality: Fresh, filtered water can make a big difference in the flavor of your tea.
Adjust Steeping Time: The longer you steep, the stronger the flavor. If you like your tea milder, steep for less time.
Ice Tea Version: To make iced tea, brew the tea as usual, let it cool, and serve over ice. You can add sweeteners or lemon to taste.
This is a basic recipe, but you can experiment with different types of teas and additions to find your perfect cup!
